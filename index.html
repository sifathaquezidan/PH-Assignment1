Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur, economist, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate. He is renowned for pioneering the concept of microcredit and microfinance, which provide small loans to impoverished individuals without requiring collateral, enabling them to establish self-sustaining businesses. Born on June 28, 1940, in Chittagong, he pursued higher education in economics at Dhaka University and later earned a PhD from Vanderbilt University.
